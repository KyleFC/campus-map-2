Grants

Concordia University's institutional aid must go towards institutional charges, such as tuition, fees, university housing, and meal plans. It does not cover university class fees, fines, etc. Academic and Athletic Scholarships and Department Awards, including need-based aid, are known as Concordia institutional aid.

Concordia University Need-Based Grants, All New Students
First Generation Grant


Award Range

	

Up to $ 2000




Requirements:

	

FAFSA required. Awarded to students who are the first in their family to attend college and demonstrate financial need.




Additional Notes:

	

Awarded to students who are the first in their family to attend college and demonstrate financial need. Renewable for 4 to 8 semesters based on class level. Students must maintain full-time status and cumulative 2.0 GPA.

Friends of Concordia


Award Range:

	

Amount determined by financial need




Requirements::

	

FAFSA required; demonstrates financial need




Additional Notes:

	Must demonstrate financial need.  Renewable for 4 to 8 semesters based on class level. Students must maintain full-time status and cumulative 2.0 GPA.
Lutheran Student Grant


Award Range:

	

Amount determined by financial need




Requirements:

	

FAFSA required demonstrates financial need




Additional Notes:

	Must demonstrate financial need.  Renewable for 4 to 8 semesters based on class level. Students must maintain full-time status and cumulative 2.0 GPA.
State and Federal Grants, All Students
Cal Grant A


Award Range:

	$9,358


Requirements:

	FAFSA filed by March 2nd. Must be a California Resident and California H.S. Graduate. GPA Verification postmarked by March 2.


Additional Notes:

	

Intended for low and middle income students who have not completed more than 9 semesters of college work. Awarded for tuition and fees only or up to FAFSA need. Based on GPA and need.

Cal Grant B


Award Range:

	

Freshman year (0-29 units): $1,648

Any other year (30+ units): $11,006




Requirements:

	FAFSA filed by March 2nd. Must be a California Resident and California H.S. Graduate. GPA Verification postmarked by March 2.


Additional Notes:

	

Intended for very low income students who have not completed more than 1 semester or 16 units of part-time college work. Awarded for tuition and fees only or up to FAFSA need. Based on GPA and need.

Federal Pell Grant


Award Range:

	

Amounts vary; click here for more information




Requirements:

	

FAFSA required.




Additional Notes:

	

Awarded to financially needy students enrolled in a Title IV school with an EFC range of 0 to 6206 as indicated on the SAR.

Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant (FSEOG)


Award Range:

	

up to $1000




Requirements:

	

FAFSA required. Must have completed financial aid file.




Additional Notes:

	

Limited funding available; awarded on a first-come, first-served basis with an EFC of 0 to 1000.

Federal Work Study


Award Range:

	

up to $4000




Requirements:

	

FAFSA required. Must have completed financial aid file




Additional Notes:

	Limited funding available. Offered on a first-come, first-served basis to students with an EFC of 0 to 1000. You must obtain a student employment position; money you earn from that position will be paid directly to you from your Federal Work Study offer.
Cal Grant Access Award for Foster Youth


Award Range:

	

up to $6,000




Requirements:

	

FAFSA required. Must have Cal A or B award




Additional Notes:

	Amount added to Cal A or B award. Student must be verified as an eligible foster youth by the California Department of Social Services.
