Borland-Manske Center Recording Studio

Completed in 2019, the BMC studio suite is one of the finest facilities of its kind in the US. Designed by renowned architect, John Storyk of Walters-Storyk Design Group, the main studio features 1100 sq. ft. of floor space including a large live room that can comfortably accommodate 25 musicians, 3 large isolation booths, an isolated amp room, a spacious control room and a producer’s office that functions as a “B” control room. An additional 5000 sq. ft. of recording space is available in the large orchestral and choral halls that add 32 audio channels for each room with remote-controlled video monitoring to the studio control room. Each hall contains a Steinway B piano.

Concordia University Irvine’s New WSDG-Designed Recording Studio Teaching Complex

Focal Point For Campus’s New Music, Worship & Theology Building

Concordia University Irvine recently completed WSDG Walters-Storyk Design Group designed recording studio/teaching facility has quickly proved itself a locus for music production education within the campus’s new 37,500 square foot Borland-Manske Center. Commissioned in 2017, and funded by private donations, the $35M complex became fully operational in the fall of 2019.
